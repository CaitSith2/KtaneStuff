{{main|2018 French Open}}

[[Rafael Nadal]] was the defending champion and successfully defended his title, defeating [[Dominic Thiem]] in the final, 6–4, 6–3, 6–2. Nadal equaled [[Margaret Court]]'s [[List of Grand Slam related tennis records#Most wins per event|all-time record of 11 Grand Slam singles titles won at one major]] and became the first player to achieve that feat in the [[History of tennis#Open era|Open era]].<ref>{{citeweb|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2018/jun/10/rafael-nadal-v-dominic-thiem-french-open-2018-mens-final-live|title =French Open 2018: Rafael Nadal beats Dominic Thiem in men's final – as it happened|date=10 June 2018|work=Guardian|accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{citeweb|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/tennis/44431333|title =French Open 2018: Rafael Nadal beats Dominic Thiem to win 11th title|date=10 June 2018|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref>	
Nadal also retained the [[List of ATP number 1 ranked singles tennis players|ATP No. 1 singles ranking]] by defending the title. Nadal and [[Roger Federer]] (despite having withdrawn from the clay season) were in contention for the top ranking at the start of the tournament.<ref>{{citeweb|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2018/jun/10/rafael-nadal-wins-eleventh-french-open-dominic-thiem|title =Rafael Nadal wins 11th French Open title with three-set victory over Thiem|date=10 June 2018|work=Guardian|accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref>	

With [[Alexander Zverev]] as the second seed, this was the first time since the [[2006 Australian Open]]&mdash;when Federer and [[Andy Roddick]] were the top 2 seeds&mdash;that a player outside of the [[Big Four (tennis)|Big Four]] was one of the top 2 seeds at a Grand Slam.

[[Novak Djokovic]]'s loss in the quarterfinal ensured a first time Grand Slam finalist from the bottom half of the draw. Thiem became the first Austrian player to reach a Grand Slam singles final since [[Thomas Muster]] at the [[1995 French Open]].

[[Marco Cecchinato]], ranked 72nd in the [[ATP rankings]], became the first unseeded men's singles semifinalist at the French Open since [[Gaël Monfils]] in [[2008 French Open – Men's Singles|2008]], the lowest-ranked male singles player to reach the French Open semifinals since [[Andriy Medvedev]] in [[1999 French Open|1999]], and the first Italian man to reach a Grand Slam singles semifinal since [[Corrado Barazzutti]] at the [[1978 French Open – Men's Singles|1978 French Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/en/news/djokovic-cecchinato-roland-garros-2018-tuesday|title=Cecchinato Stuns Djokovic In Roland Garros Thriller|date=5 June 2018|publisher=ATP World Tour}}</ref>
