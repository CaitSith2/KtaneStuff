{{main|2018 Wimbledon Championships}}

[[Roger Federer]] was the top seed and defending champion, but lost in the quarterfinals to [[Kevin Anderson (tennis)|Kevin Anderson]]. 

[[Novak Djokovic]] claimed his fourth Wimbledon title, defeating Anderson in the final 6–2, 6–2, 7–6<sup>(7–3)</sup>. The win moved him to 13 grand slam titles, and outright fourth place on the [[List of Grand Slam men's singles champions#Most Grand Slam singles titles (5 or more)|all time men's singles grand slam wins list]], passing [[Roy Emerson]]. The win was also Djokovic's first title on the ATP tour for over 12 months, his last win coming at [[2017 Aegon International Eastbourne|Eastbourne]] on July 1, 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/44839313 |title=Novak Djokovic wins fourth Wimbledon by beating Kevin Anderson |first1=Jonathan |last1=Jurejko |date=15 July 2018 |publisher=[[BBC Sport]] |access-date=15 July 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{citeweb|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2018/jul/15/novak-djokovic-wins-fourth-wimbledon-title-kevin-anderson |title =Novak Djokovic seals fourth Wimbledon title in final stroll over Kevin Anderson|date=15 July 2018|work=Guardian|accessdate=17 July 2018}}</ref>	

The semifinal match between Anderson and [[John Isner]], lasting 6 hours 36 minutes, was [[Longest tennis match records#Men's singles|the second longest men's singles match at Wimbledon and the third longest men's singles match in tennis history]]. Isner has thus played in the two longest matches in Wimbledon history (the other one being the record-holding [[Isner–Mahut match at the 2010 Wimbledon Championships|2010 match against Nicolas Mahut]]).<ref>http://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2018/07/the-latest-mens-wimbledon-semifinals-underway/75276/</ref> The 2018 semifinals were the longest two semifinals in Wimbledon history.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.eurosport.com/tennis/wimbledon/2017/novak-djokovic-pips-rafael-nadal-in-second-longest-wimbledon-semi-final-ever_sto6847260/story.shtml |title=Novak Djokovic pips Rafael Nadal in second longest Wimbledon semi-final ever |first1=Dan |last1=Quarrell |date=14 July 2018 |publisher=[[Eurosport]] |access-date=15 July 2018 }}</ref>

Anderson became the first man representing South Africa to reach the Wimbledon men's singles final since [[Brian Norton]] in [[1921 Wimbledon Championships - Men's Singles|1921]] (South African-born [[Kevin Curren]] represented the United States when he was a finalist in [[1985 Wimbledon Championships - Men's Singles|1985]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.atpworldtour.com/en/news/anderson-isner-wimbledon-2018-friday|title=Anderson Wins 26-24 In Fifth Set, Reaches Wimbledon Final|website=atpworldtour.com|date=July 13, 2018}}</ref>

Federer and [[Rafael Nadal]] were in contention for the [[List of ATP number 1 ranked singles tennis players|ATP No. 1 ranking]] at the start of the tournament. Nadal retained the top ranking by reaching the fourth round. 

[[Feliciano López]] made his 66th consecutive Grand Slam main draw appearance, surpassing [[List of Grand Slam related tennis records#Participation|the previous record]] of 65 consecutive appearances he jointly held with Federer.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/sport/tennis/tennis-del-potro-shows-his-form-in-three-set-wimbledon-romp-past-lopez|title=Tennis: Del Potro shows his form in three-set Wimbledon romp past Lopez|website=The Straits Times|date=July 5, 2018}}</ref>
