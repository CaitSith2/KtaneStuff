{{main|2017 Wimbledon Championships}}

[[Andy Murray]] was the defending champion, and top seed, but lost to [[Sam Querrey]] in the quarterfinals. Despite his loss, Murray retained the [[List of ATP number 1 ranked singles tennis players|ATP No. 1 singles ranking]] at the end of the tournament, as [[Rafael Nadal]], [[Stan Wawrinka]], and [[Novak Djokovic]] all failed to gain enough ranking points to surpass him.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/en/news/murray-nadal-djokovic-federer-wimbledon-2017-draw|title=Murray, Nadal In Same Half Of Wimbledon Draw|publisher=ATP World Tour|date=30 June 2017}}</ref>

[[Roger Federer]] won the title, achieving his unprecedented  eighth Wimbledon Gentleman's Singles Title and 19th Grand Slam men's singles title, defeating [[Marin Čilić]] in the final, 6–3, 6–1, 6–4.<ref>{{citeweb|url= https://www.nytimes.com/2017/07/16/sports/tennis/wimbledon-roger-federer-marin-cilic.html|title= Roger Federer Wins Record-Breaking Eighth Wimbledon Title|date=16 July 2017|work=New York Times|accessdate=17 July 2017}}</ref> Federer thus became the only player to win the Gentleman's singles title eight times, surpassing Pete Sampras and William Renshaw, who had each won it seven times. <ref>{{citeweb|url= https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/sports/wp/2017/07/16/roger-federer-at-35-wins-wimbledon-for-an-eighth-time/|title=Roger Federer, at 35, wins Wimbledon for a record eighth time|date=16 July 2017|work=The Washington Post|accessdate=19 July 2017}}</ref> Federer also became only the second man in the Open era, after [[Björn Borg]] in [[1976 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|1976]], to win Wimbledon without losing a set. This was Federer's 70th appearance at a Grand Slam, [[List of Grand Slam related tennis records#Participation|tying the record for male players]] and a record-breaking 11th men's singles final at the same Grand Slam tournament.<ref>{{citeweb|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2017/jul/16/wimbledon-2017-roger-federer-v-marin-cilic-in-mens-singles-final-live|title= Wimbledon 2017: Roger Federer beats Marin Cilic to seal record eighth title – as it happened|date=16 July 2017|work=Guardian|accessdate=17 July 2017}}</ref> In addition, by virtue of his third-round win over [[Mischa Zverev]], Federer won his [[Open Era tennis records – men's singles#Grand Slam tournaments|317th Grand Slam singles match]], surpassing [[Serena Williams]]' [[Open Era tennis records – women's singles#Grand Slam tournaments|record of 316 match wins]] and giving him the all-time record for the most Grand Slam singles wins by any player, male or female. The tournament marked the fifth time that Nadal and Federer won the French Open and Wimbledon respectively in the same year.<ref>{{citeweb|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/tennis/40625069|title=Wimbledon 2017: Roger Federer beats Marin Cilic to win record eighth title|date=16 July 2017|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=17 July 2017}}</ref>	

This was also the first Grand Slam tournament since the [[2009 French Open – Men's Singles|2009 French Open]] in which Murray, Nadal and Djokovic all failed to reach the semifinals, and the first time since 2004 that no player reached [[All-time tennis records – men's singles#Four_Majors|all 4 quarterfinals]] in a year. With his win over Murray, Querrey became the first American man to reach a Grand Slam semifinal since [[Andy Roddick]] at the [[2009 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2009 Wimbledon Championships]]  and the second time to beat a world No 1 player, the first was Djokovic at the [[2016 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2016 Wimbledon Championships]].   For the first time since the [[2005 Australian Open – Men's Singles|2005 Australian Open]], [[David Ferrer]] was not seeded at a Grand Slam and the first time since the [[2004 US Open – Men's Singles|2004 US Open]] that fellow Spaniard [[Guillermo García López]] did not play at a Grand Slam, ending his [[List of Grand Slam related tennis records#Participation|streak of 50 consecutive Grand Slam appearances]].<ref>{{citeweb|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2017/jul/17/roger-federer-wimbledon-record-class-apart|title= Roger Federer stands tallest of all as Wimbledon’s history man|date=16 July 2017|work=Guardian|accessdate=17 July 2017}}</ref>
