{{main|2012 US Open (tennis)}}

[[Andy Murray]] defeated the defending champion from 2011, [[Novak Djokovic]] in the [[2012 US Open &ndash; Men's Singles final|final]]. The final lasted 4 hours and 54 minutes, in a record-equaling US Open final in the [[Open Era]]. Murray defeated Djokovic 7–6<sup>(12–10)</sup>, 7–5, 2–6, 3–6, 6–2 to win his first Grand Slam title, becoming the first British man since [[Fred Perry]], winner of the US Open in [[1936 U.S. National Championships – Men's Singles|1936]], to win a Grand Slam championship, after having been runner-up on four previous occasions. Murray also won the [[2004 US Open – Boys' Singles|junior title]] in 2004.

[[Andy Roddick]], the 2003 champion, played his last professional match here before retirement, losing to [[Juan Martín del Potro]] in the fourth round and bringing a close to his 13-year career.

The 2012 tournament marked the first time since the [[2004 French Open]] that neither [[Roger Federer]] nor [[Rafael Nadal]] reached the semifinals of a Grand Slam event.

This tournament ended the streak of five years in which Djokovic and Federer faced each other at the US Open.
