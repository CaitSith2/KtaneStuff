{{main|2005 Wimbledon Championships}}
[[Roger Federer]] successfully defended his title again, winning Wimbledon for a third consecutive year by defeating [[Andy Roddick]] in the final for the second year in a row, 6–2, 7–6<sup>(7–2)</sup>, 6–4 to win the Gentlemen's Singles tennis title at the 2005 Wimbledon Championships.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref>

This tournament was the first Grand Slam tournament in which future [[List of ATP number 1 ranked singles tennis players|World No. 1]] and Wimbledon champions [[Andy Murray]] and [[Novak Djokovic]] competed in the main draw. Both players were defeated in the third round: Djokovic was defeated by [[Sébastien Grosjean]] in four sets, while Murray was defeated by [[David Nalbandian]] after leading two sets to love; to date it remains the only professional match in which Murray has lost from two sets up.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Cheese |first1=Caroline |title=Brave Murray falls to Nalbandian|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/4619129.stm |website=bbc.co.uk |publisher=''[[BBC]]'' |accessdate=7 June 2018 |date=25 June 2005}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Andy Murray makes history at Wimbledon |url=https://www.theroar.com.au/2013/07/09/andy-murray-makes-history-at-wimbledon/|website=theroar.com.au|publisher=''The Roar''|accessdate=7 June 2018|date=9 July 2013}}</ref>
<br/>Novak Djokovic entered ATP Top 100 for the first time after this tournament.
