{{main article|2017 US Open (tennis)}}

[[Angelique Kerber]] was the defending champion, but lost in the first round to [[Naomi Osaka]]. Kerber became the second US Open defending champion to lose in the first round after [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]] in [[2005 US Open – Women's Singles|2005]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/08/29/sports/tennis/naomi-osaka-angelique-kerber-us-open.html|title=Naomi Osaka Ousts Angelique Kerber, the Defending Champion, at the U.S. Open|date=29 August 2017|work=The New York Times|author=Ben Rothenberg|accessdate=5 September 2017}}</ref>

[[Sloane Stephens]] won her first Grand Slam title, defeating [[Madison Keys]] in the final, 6–3, 6–0.<ref>{{citeweb|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/41216153|title= Sloane Stephens beats Madison Keys to win US Open|date=9 September 2017|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=9 September 2017}}</ref> It was the first all-American women's final at the US Open since [[2002 US Open – Women's Singles|2002]], and the second time in three years that the final featured two first-time Grand Slam singles finalists from the same country. Stephens became the second unseeded woman in the [[Open Era]] to win the US Open after [[Kim Clijsters]] in [[2009 US Open – Women's Singles|2009]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2017-09-09/2017-09-09_day_13_recap_stephens_stands_tall_a_us_open_champion.html|title=Day 13 Recap: Stephens stands tall, a US Open champion|date=9 September 2017|work=usopen.org|author=E.J. Crawford|accessdate=10 September 2017}}</ref><ref>{{citeweb|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2017/sep/09/sloane-stephens-madison-keys-us-open-title|title= Sloane Stephens surges past Madison Keys to win US Open title|date=10 September 2017|work=Guardian|accessdate=19 September 2017}}</ref>	

For the first time since [[1981 US Open – Women's Singles|1981]] at the US Open&mdash;and at any Grand Slam event since the [[1985 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|1985 Wimbledon Championships]]&mdash;all four semifinalists were American. Both occasions were also the last time that four players from the same country accomplished these feats.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/09/06/sports/tennis/us-open-results.html?mcubz=3|title=U.S. Open: Madison Keys Completes All-American Semifinals; Nadal Advances|date=6 September 2017|work=The New York Times|author=Christopher Clarey and Ben Rothenberg|accessdate=6 September 2017}}</ref> In addition, [[Kaia Kanepi]] became the first qualifier to reach the US Open quarterfinals since [[Barbara Gerken]], also in 1981.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/09/04/sports/tennis/coco-vandeweghe-karolina-pliskova-us-open.html|title=CoCo Vandeweghe’s Hard Work Is Taking Her Further|date=4 September 2017|work=The New York Times|author=Scott Cacciola|accessdate=5 September 2017}}</ref>

This was [[Maria Sharapova]]'s first Grand Slam appearance since the [[2016 Australian Open]], following a suspension for violating the anti-doping rules. Sharapova was awarded a wildcard into the main draw and advanced to the fourth round, where she lost to [[Anastasija Sevastova]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/41143480|title=Maria Sharapova knocked out of US Open by Anastasija Sevastova|date=4 September 2017|work=BBC Sport|author=Piers Newbery|accessdate=5 September 2017}}</ref>

[[Garbiñe Muguruza]] became the new [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|WTA No. 1 player]] at the end of the tournament despite losing in the fourth round, after [[Karolína Plíšková]] (who held the top ranking at the start of the tournament) failed to defend her finalist points from 2016. Eight of the top nine seeds (with the exception of Kerber) were in contention for the no. 1 ranking at the start of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/battle-no1-eight-players-set-fight-top-spot-us-open|title= Battle for No.1: Eight players set to fight for top spot at US Open|date=23 August 2017|work=Women's Tennis Association|author=Courtney Nguyen|accessdate=23 August 2017}}</ref>

Venus Williams became the first player in history to reach the semifinals of the US Open 10 years apart and then do so another 10 years apart (1997, 2007, and 2017).
