{{main|2013 US Open (tennis)}}

[[Andy Murray]] was the defending champion, but lost in the quarterfinals  to [[Stan Wawrinka]].

[[Rafael Nadal]] won the title, defeating [[Novak Djokovic]] in the final to claim a second US Open title and win his thirteenth grand slam. The win moved him to 13 grand slam titles, and moved to third place on the [[List of Grand Slam men's singles champions#Most Grand Slam singles titles (5 or more)|all time men's singles Grand Slam wins list]], passing [[Roy Emerson]].<ref>{{citeweb|url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/24027678|title=Rafael Nadal beats Novak Djokovic to win second US Open title|date=10 September 2013|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref><ref>{{citeweb|url= http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sport/tennis/article-2416509/Rafael-Nadal-beats-Novak-Djokovic-win-US-Open.html?ito=feeds-newsxml|title=Lucky No 13 for Rafa as stunning Spaniard destroys Djokovic in four sets to claim second US Open title at Flushing Meadows|date=10 September 2013|work=Daily Mail|accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref>

Both Djokovic and Nadal were in contention for the [[List of ATP number 1 ranked singles players#ATP_Rankings|World No. 1 ranking]] at the start of the tournament<ref>{{citeweb|url= https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/2013/09/09/us-open-2013-rafael-nadal-novak-djokovic-mens-final/2790623/|title=Nadal takes down Djokovic to win U.S. Open|date=10 September 2013|work=USA Today|accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref> but Djokovic retained the top position by reaching the final.<ref>{{citeweb|url= http://espn.go.com/tennis/usopen13/story/_/id/9656100/2013-us-open-rafael-nadal-bests-novak-djokovic-title-caps-stellar-13|title=Rafael Nadal wins 13th Slam title|date=10 September 2013|work=ESPN|accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref>

[[Roger Federer]]'s streak of nine consecutive US Open quarterfinals ended when he lost to [[Tommy Robredo]] in the fourth round, marking his earliest exit from the tournament since [[2003 US Open – Men's Singles|2003]].
