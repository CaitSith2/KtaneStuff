{{main|2011 Wimbledon Championships}}
[[Novak Djokovic]] defeated the defending champion [[Rafael Nadal]] in the final, 6–4, 6–1, 1–6, 6–3, to win the Gentlemen's Singles tennis title at the 2011 Wimbledon Championships.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref> It was his first Wimbledon and third Grand Slam title, having previously won the [[2008 Australian Open – Men's Singles|2008]] and [[2011 Australian Open – Men's Singles|2011 Australian Open]].

In losing to Djokovic in the final, Nadal ended his 20-match Wimbledon winning streak dating back to [[2008 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2008]], having missed the [[2009 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2009 championships]] due to injury. This was also the first time since [[2002 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2002]] that neither [[Roger Federer]] nor Nadal had won Wimbledon.

Both Nadal and Djokovic were in contention for the [[List of ATP number 1 ranked singles tennis players|ATP no. 1 singles ranking]] at the start of the tournament for the second time. By winning his semifinal match against [[Jo-Wilfried Tsonga]] to reach the final, Djokovic gained the World No. 1 ranking for the first time, which marked the first time that neither Federer nor Nadal was ranked No. 1 since 2 February 2004.

This Grand Slam was two-time French Open finalist [[Robin Söderling]]'s last Grand Slam before being diagnosed with [[mononucleosis]] and eventually retiring from tennis.
