{{main|2013 Wimbledon Championships}}
[[Roger Federer]] was the defending champion but he lost in the second round in a huge upset to World No. 116 [[Sergiy Stakhovsky]] from [[Ukraine]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/23074912|title=Roger Federer suffers earliest Wimbledon exit since 2002|work=[[BBC News]]|date=28 June 2013|accessdate=28 June 2013}}</ref> Stakhovsky was unable to build on his effort and lost in the next round in 4 sets to [[Jürgen Melzer]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://espn.go.com/tennis/wimbledon13/story/_/id/9432802/wimbledon-sergiy-stakhovsky-magic-comes-end=|title=Stakhovsky's magic comes to end|work=ESPN Tennis|date=28 June 2013|accessdate=28 June 2013}}</ref> Federer's loss marked the first time since the [[2004 French Open – Men's Singles|2004 French Open]], when he lost to [[Gustavo Kuerten]] in the [[2004 French Open – Men's Singles#Section 1|third round]], that he had failed to reach at least the quarterfinals in a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] event. It was also Federer's earliest exit in this tournament since [[2002 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2002]], and the first time that Federer lost to a player ranked lower than 100 since his loss to then World No. 101 [[Richard Gasquet]] at the [[2005 Monte Carlo Masters – Singles|2005 Monte Carlo Masters]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/tennis/players/top-players/roger-federer.aspx?t=pa&y=2005&m=s&e=410#|title=2005 Federer Playing Activity|work=ATP World Tour Website|date=27 June 2013|accessdate=27 June 2013}}</ref>

In the first round, World No. 135 [[Steve Darcis]] had already produced another of the biggest upsets in recent years by defeating World No. 5 and two-time Wimbledon champion [[Rafael Nadal]] in straight sets. This came a year after Nadal's equally big upset in the second round of the [[2012 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2012 Championships]] against [[Lukáš Rosol]]. It was the first time ever that Nadal had lost in the first round of a Grand Slam tournament.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2013/jun/24/rafael-nadal-steve-darcis-wimbledon-2013|title=Rafael Nadal knocked out of Wimbledon by Belgian Steve Darcis|date=25 June 2013|work=The Guardian|accessdate=25 June 2013 }}</ref> There was no follow-up match from Darcis as he withdrew from the tournament because of a right shoulder injury he sustained during the Nadal match.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2013/jun/26/steve-darcis-withdraws-wimbledon|title=Wimbledon 2013: Steve Darcis withdraws with shoulder injury|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=26 June 2013|accessdate=26 June 2013}}</ref> Federer and Nadal both exiting the tournament early made this the first time since the [[2004 French Open]] that neither of them appeared in the quarterfinals of a grand slam.

Further history was made at the championships for American and Polish players: for the first time since the [[1912 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|1912 Wimbledon Championships]] no American men advanced past the second round;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/2013/06/27/tennis-wimbledon-americans-idUKL3N0F33JU20130627|title=Tennis-American men suffer worst Wimbledon in over 100 years|work=[[Reuters]]|date=27 June 2013|accessdate=28 June 2013}}</ref> and for the first time ever a Polish man made it to the semifinal stage of a Grand Slam after [[Jerzy Janowicz]] defeated countryman [[Łukasz Kubot]] in the quarterfinals.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2013/jul/03/jerzy-janowicz-lukasz-kubot-wimbledon|title=Wimbledon 2013: Jerzy Janowicz pounds past fellow Pole Lukasz Kubot|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=3 July 2013|accessdate=6 July 2013}}</ref>

[[Andy Murray]] defeated [[Novak Djokovic]] in the [[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Men's singles final|final]] in straight sets, 6–4, 7–5, 6–4 to win the Gentlemen's Singles tennis title at the 2013 Wimbledon Championships, becoming the first British man to win the Wimbledon singles title since [[Fred Perry]] in [[1936 Wimbledon Championships - Men's Singles|1936]], ending a 76-year drought,{{#tag:ref|Although commonly reported as a 77-year drought – as there are 77 years between Perry's 1936 win and Murray's 2013 win – Perry remained the Wimbledon Men's Singles champion until the 1937 Championships.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/blogs-magazine-monitor-23225058 |title=Andy Murray: Is it really 77 years since Wimbledon was won by a British man? |work=[[BBC News]] |date=8 July 2013 |accessdate=8 July 2013 }}</ref>|group="A"|name="76-year drought"}} as well as the first British winner in singles since [[Virginia Wade]] won the women's event in 1977. He was also the first [[Scottish people|Scot]] to win the Wimbledon title since [[Harold Mahony]] in [[1896 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|1896]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/23217393|title=Andy Murray beats Novak Djokovic to win Wimbledon|work=[[BBC News]]|date=7 July 2013|accessdate=7 July 2013}}</ref> Djokovic had advanced to the final after an epic and gruelling five-set semifinal against [[Juan Martín del Potro]]. At 4 hours and 43 minutes this match made history as the longest semifinal ever contested at Wimbledon, breaking the previous record set by [[Boris Becker]] and [[Ivan Lendl]] at the [[1989 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|1989 Championships]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/23200853|title=Novak Djokovic beats del Potro to reach Wimbledon final|work=[[BBC News]]|date=5 July 2013|accessdate=5 July 2013}}</ref>
