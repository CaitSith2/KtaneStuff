{{main|2009 French Open}}

[[Rafael Nadal]] was the four-time defending champion, but lost in the fourth round to [[Robin Söderling]] in a huge upset. This was Nadal's first defeat (of two) at the French Open, having won the title in all four of his previous appearances at Roland Garros since debuting in [[2005 French Open &ndash; Men's Singles|2005]]. With Nadal's loss, there would be a first time French Open champion for 2009.

[[Roger Federer]] beat Söderling in the final 6–1, 7–6<sup>(7–1)</sup>, 6–4 to win his first French Open title, complete the [[Grand Slam (tennis)#Career Grand Slam|career Grand Slam]], and equal [[Pete Sampras]]' then-record of 14 Grand Slam titles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/tennis/8087015.stm|title=Federer claims historic Paris win|publisher=BBC Sport|first=David|last=Ornstein|date=7 June 2009|accessdate=25 January 2016}}</ref>

Until the [[2016 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2016 Wimbledon]], this was the last Grand Slam tournament that [[Novak Djokovic]] failed to reach the quarter-finals. He lost to [[Philipp Kohlschreiber]] in the third round.<ref name="Djokovic">[http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/novak-djokovic-crashes-out-of-french-open/story-e6frf9if-1225718656401 Novak Djokovic crashes out of French Open | Herald Sun]</ref>
