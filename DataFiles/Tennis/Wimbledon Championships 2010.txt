{{main|2010 Wimbledon Championships}}
[[Roger Federer]] was the defending champion but lost in the quarterfinals to [[Tomáš Berdych]]. This was the first time since 2003 that Federer did not make the Wimbledon men's singles final, and the second successive Grand Slam tournament that Federer was defeated at the quarterfinal stage, having previously been on a record run of 23 successive Grand Slam semifinals (of which he won all but three). Federer's loss, along those with [[Lleyton Hewitt]] and [[Andy Roddick]] were out at the fourth round, ensured that will have a first time Wimbledon finalist from the top half of the draw.

[[Rafael Nadal]], the world No. 1 and second seed, defeated 12th seed Berdych in the final, 6–3, 7–5, 6–4, to win the Gentlemen's Singles tennis title at the 2010 Wimbledon Championships, his second Wimbledon title and his eighth Grand Slam title overall.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2010/jul/04/wimbledon-2010-rafael-nadal-wins|title=Rafael Nadal claims title with win over Tomas Berdych|date=2010-07-04|work=The Guardian|accessdate=18 November 2010}}</ref> It was Nadal's 4th non-consecutive Wimbledon final as he skipped the [[2009 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2009 tournament]] because of injuries.

Berdych became the first Czech man to reach the final since [[Ivan Lendl]]'s appearance in the [[1987 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|1987 final]]. For the first time ever there were no [[England|English]] players in the Wimbledon men's singles competition, however Great Britain was represented by two players from [[Scotland]]: fourth seed [[Andy Murray]] and wildcard [[Jamie Baker (tennis)|Jamie Baker]].<ref>{{cite news|publisher=BBC |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/8745332.stm|title=Historic first as no English men in Wimbledon draw|date=17 June 2010|accessdate=17 June 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100620182726/http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/8745332.stm| archivedate= 20 June 2010 <!--DASHBot-->| deadurl= no}}</ref>

The [[Isner–Mahut match at the 2010 Wimbledon Championships|first round match]] between [[John Isner]] and [[Nicolas Mahut]] set a new record for the longest tennis match in history (in both time and total of games), as well as many other records due to its length. Isner won the match, taking the final set 70–68 after a total of 11 hours and 5 minutes play across three days.<ref>{{cite news|title=Isner finally comes out on top in longest match ever|agency=Reuters  |date=24 June 2010|accessdate=24 June 2010|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/idUKLDE65N1PL20100624}}</ref> The two faced each other again in the first round the following year, with Isner taking that match 7–6<sup>(7–4)</sup>, 6–2, 7–6<sup>(8–6)</sup>.
