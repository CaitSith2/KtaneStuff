[[Stefan Edberg]] was the defending champion and retained his title, defeating [[Pete Sampras]] 3–6, 6–4, 7–6<sup>(7–5)</sup>, 6–2 in the final to win the men's singles title at the [[1992 US Open (tennis)|1992 US Open]]. The semifinal between Edberg and [[Michael Chang]] was a battle wherein Edberg won in five sets after 5 hours and 26 minutes, then the longest match in the Open Era. This is also where [[John McEnroe]] and [[Jimmy Connors]] played their last Grand Slams.
