{{main article|2018 US Open (tennis)}}

[[Rafael Nadal]] was the defending champion, but retired in his semifinal match against [[Juan Martín del Potro]].

[[Novak Djokovic]] won the title, his third at the US Open and [[List_of_Grand_Slam_related_tennis_records#Men|14th Grand Slam title overall]], tying [[Pete Sampras]], defeating Juan Martín del Potro in the final, 6–3, 7–6<sup>(7–4)</sup>, 6–3.<ref>{{citeweb|url= https://www.bbc.com/sport/tennis/45468005|title =Novak Djokovic beats Juan Martin del Potro to win title|date=9 September 2018|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=10 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{citeweb|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2018/sep/09/novak-djokovic-juan-martin-del-potro-us-open-mens-final-match-report |title =Novak Djokovic powers past Juan Martín del Potro to win third US Open|date=10 September 2018|work=Guardian|accessdate=10 September 2018}}</ref>	
	
Nadal and [[Roger Federer]] were in contention for the [[List of ATP number 1 ranked singles tennis players|ATP no. 1 singles ranking]] at the start of the tournament. Nadal retained the number 1 ranking after Federer lost in the fourth round.

This tournament marks former world no. 3 [[David Ferrer]]'s last participation in a Grand Slam tournament; he retired from his first round match against Nadal due to injury. This was also the last Grand Slam tournament for [[Julien Benneteau]], [[Nicolas Mahut]], [[Gilles Müller]], [[Florian Mayer]] and former world no. 8 and two-time US Open semifinalist [[Mikhail Youzhny]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tennisworldusa.org/tennis/news/Rafael_Nadal/59437/david-ferrer-it-will-be-my-last-us-open-facing-rafael-nadal-is-a-gift-/ |title =David Ferrer: 'It will be my last US Open. Facing Rafael Nadal is a gift'|date=26 August 2018|work=Tennisworldusa|accessdate=28 August 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/tennis/story/_/id/24495658/us-open-men-first-round-roundup-monday-results |title =Andy Murray advances; David Ferrer retires in final Grand Slam match|date=27 August 2018|work=ESPN|accessdate=28 August 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.atpworldtour.com/en/news/mayer-muller-us-open-2018-retirements|title= Mayer, Muller Retires on Open's Opening Day|date=27 August 2018|accessdate=29 August 2018|work=ATP}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://au.sports.yahoo.com/retiring-tennis-dads-heartwarming-moment-son-us-open-loss-223246875.html |title=Nicolas Mahut's heartwarming moment with son at US Open |publisher=Au.sports.yahoo.com |date=2018-08-24 |accessdate=2018-08-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tennisworldusa.org/tennis/news/Tennis_Stories/53467/julien-benneteau-to-end-his-career-this-year-here-is-where/ |title=Julien Benneteau to end his career this year - Here is where |author=Luigi Gatto|publisher=tennisworldusa.org |date=2018-04-05 |accessdate=2018-09-10}}</ref>

[[Peter Polansky]] became the first player in history to qualify as a lucky loser for the main draw of all four Grand Slams in the same calendar year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2018-08-24/2018-08-24_peter_polansky_gets_fourth_consecutive_lucky_loser_spot.html |title=Peter Polansky gets fourth consecutive 'lucky loser' spot |author=Ashley Marshall|publisher=usopen.org |date=2018-08-24 |accessdate=2018-09-10}}</ref>

After [[Juan Martín del Potro]] and [[Marin Čilić]] turn 30 in September, there will be no Grand Slam holder under 30 years old.
