{{main|2010 French Open}}
[[Roger Federer]] was the defending champion, but lost in the quarterfinals to [[Robin Söderling]], which allowed the Swede to make a second successive final here. This was the first time since the [[2004 French Open – Men's Singles|2004 French Open]] that Federer did not reach the semifinals of a Grand Slam tournament. Between the 2004 French Open and the [[2017 French Open – Men's Singles|2017 French Open]], this was the only Grand Slam not to feature either Federer or [[Novak Djokovic]] in the semifinals.<ref>{{cite web|title=Söderling Stuns Federer For Semi-Final Berth; Battle For No. 1 Intensifies|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/News/Tennis/2010/05/Roland-Garros/Roland-Garros-Tuesday2-Soderling-Stuns-Federer.aspx|publisher=[[ATP World Tour]]|accessdate=4 June 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100602233947/http://www.atpworldtour.com/News/Tennis/2010/05/Roland-Garros/Roland-Garros-Tuesday2-Soderling-Stuns-Federer.aspx| archivedate= 2 June 2010 <!--DASHBot-->| deadurl= no}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Rockin’ Robin repeats his feat of clay |url=http://www.rolandgarros.com/en_FR/news/articles/2010-06-01/201006011275424112496.html |publisher=Roland Garros.com |accessdate=4 June 2010 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100604051920/http://www.rolandgarros.com/en_FR/news/articles/2010-06-01/201006011275424112496.html |archivedate=4 June 2010 |deadurl=yes |df= }}</ref>

[[Rafael Nadal]] won his fifth French Open title by defeating Söderling in the final, 6–4, 6–2, 6–4.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/tennis/8724597.stm|title=Inspired Rafael Nadal powers to fifth French Open title |date=2010-06-06|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=2010-11-18| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20101223063248/http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/tennis/8724597.stm| archivedate= 23 December 2010 <!--DASHBot-->| deadurl= no}}</ref> Nadal finished the tournament without having dropped a set for the second time (the first time was in 2008). By winning the title, Nadal regained the [[List of ATP number 1 ranked singles tennis players|ATP no. 1 singles ranking]] from Federer, but was in contention to keep the ranking that would break [[Pete Sampras]]' record of 286 weeks as the number 1 ranking.
