{{main|1993 Wimbledon Championships}}
[[Andre Agassi]] was the defending champion but lost in the quarterfinals to [[Pete Sampras]].

Sampras defeated [[Jim Courier]] in the final, 7–6<sup>(7–3)</sup>, 7–6<sup>(8–6)</sup>, 3–6, 6–3 to win the Gentlemen's Singles tennis title at the 1993 Wimbledon Championships.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref> This was the first of Sampras's seven Wimbledon titles which was a joint  all time record with William Renshaw, which stood until the [[2017 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2017 championships]] when [[Roger Federer]] surpassed the record by winning his 8th title.
