[[Chris Evert]] defeated the unseeded [[Mima Jaušovec]] 6–1, 6–2 in the final to win the '''Women's Singles''' tennis title at the [[1983 French Open]].<ref name=collins>{{cite book|last=Collins|first=Bud|title=The Bud Collins History of Tennis|year=2010|publisher=New Chapter Press|location=[New York]|isbn=978-0942257700|edition=2nd|page=397}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author1=Morley Myers|title=Chris Evert Lloyd reclaimed her crown as the clay...|url=http://www.upi.com/Archives/1983/06/04/Chris-Evert-Lloyd-reclaimed-her-crown-as-the-clay/2533423547200/|publisher=[[UPI]]|date=4 June 1983}}</ref>

Top seed [[Martina Navratilova]] was upset in the fourth round by [[Kathy Horvath]]. It was her only loss for the entire year.

This tournament was also notable for having 128 players involved in the 1st round for the first time, and for being the first Grand Slam appearance of future World No. 1 [[Steffi Graf]]. At 13 years, 11 months and 9 days, Graf became the youngest player to make it in the main draw of Grand Slam.

It would be [[2017 French Open – Women's Singles|34 years]] before [[Jelena Ostapenko]] became the next unseeded woman to reach the final.<ref>https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2017/jun/08/french-open-jelena-ostapenko-timea-bacsinszky</ref>
