{{main|1968 French Open}}
[[Françoise Dürr]] was the defending champion but was defeated in the fourth round by [[Gail Chanfreau|Gail Sherriff]].

[[Nancy Richey]] defeated [[Ann Jones (tennis)|Ann Jones]] in the final, 5–7, 6–4, 6–1 to win the title.<ref name=collins>{{cite book|last=Collins|first=Bud|title=The Bud Collins History of Tennis|year=2010|publisher=New Chapter Press|location=[New York]|isbn=978-0942257700|edition=2nd|page=396}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author1=Simon Cambers |title=Richey recalls Roland-Garros triumph |url=https://www.rolandgarros.com/en-us/article/richey-recalls-roland-garros-triumph-1968 |website=www.rolandgarros.com |publisher=[[Fédération Française de Tennis]] (FFT) |date=9 June 2018}}</ref> It was Richey's first French open title and second (and last) Grand Slam title after the [[1967 Australian Championships – Women's Singles|1967 Australian Championships]]. The tournament was the first Grand Slam tournament of the [[History of tennis#Open Era|Open Era]], in which professionals were allowed to compete against amateurs.
